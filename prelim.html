Prelim Journey

During my Preliminary journey, the first thing I learned is Artificial Intelligence. I discovered what it's for, how it's applied in the industry and our daily lives, and what amazed me most—AI's ability for visual perception, like seeing and interpreting environments and even generating images from prompts, as in ChatGPT. Most of what we covered was about Natural Language Processing and Machine Learning. Machine learning, as I understand, is a type of AI that doesn't need a programmer to code every action; it can be told what to do in different scenarios. I learned that NLP lets computers understand human language, like words and sentences, so they can read, listen, and even talk back, like in voice assistants. NLP powers chatbots, translation apps, and spell-checkers, so it feels like they "get" what we're saying and can even entertain us when we're lonely.

Prof. Raga explained text preprocessing steps, like lowercasing, removing special characters, and tokenizing words, which can feel tedious but is still fun. I also learned how to transform text into numbers, known as Text Representation - One Hot Encoding, which is useful if an algorithm shows bias. I got familiar with Named Entity Recognition (NER), which identifies things like names, places, and dates in text—something I find very useful. My favorite topic is text summarization, a widely used NLP task that feels mind-boggling. These are the topics that stood out the most to me so far.
