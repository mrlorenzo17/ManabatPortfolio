Personal Reflection

In this exercise, we were task to train various NLP models and evaluate their performance based on different amounts and quality of training data that where the results are recorded in an excel sheet. For the model I used nlp = spacy.load("en_core_web_sm"), using the datasets i created that is split into several subset like:

  • Subset 1: Small size, low-quality (minimal preprocessing)
  • Subset 2: Medium size, low-quality
  • Subset 3: Large size, low-quality
  • Subset 4: Small size, high-quality (proper preprocessing and cleaning)
  • Subset 5: Large size, high-quality

Recording the accuracy, precision, recall, and F1 score for each model and data subset. The results for each are recorded in excel sheets.

Results Analysis

Named Entity Recognition (NER)

The spaCy NER model showed strong performance, hitting near-perfect scores (precision, recall, F1-score close to 1.0) on most subsets, no matter the data size or quality. This shows the model's skill in handling named entity recognition, even with small or noisy data. Strangely, there was a bit of a performance drop with the big, high-quality dataset, which could mean overfitting or trouble dealing with the complexity of a large, very clean dataset.

Part-of-Speech (POS) Tagging

The spaCy POS tagging model had high scores in accuracy, precision, recall, and F1-score, mostly above 0.90 across all subsets. This confirms it does POS tagging well, even with smaller or messier data. Performance hit its highest with the big, clean dataset, showing that more and better data really helps.

Sentiment Analysis

The sentiment analysis model acted unexpectedly, with scores that jumped around. While it got perfect scores on some subsets, it totally missed on others, scoring zero in precision, recall, and F1-score. This might be due to things like class imbalance in the datasets, limits of the simple bag-of-words approach, or issues in the manually created data.
