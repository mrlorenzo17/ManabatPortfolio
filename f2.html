Personal Reflection

Each of us was given a specific assignment to complete in this section of the project, which was to contribute to the Collaborative Literature Review. Everyone was expected to look for at least three related studies or works that were pertinent to our main topic. But instead of meeting the bare minimum, I chose to review six relevant works of literature. I started by looking for papers and studies that had a direct bearing on the subject of our investigation. Sorting through innumerable journals, publications, and reports was a thorough procedure. Every article I chose had to satisfy certain requirements, like being pertinent to the research, having a reliable source, and being in line with our goals. The goal of this endeavor was to make sure that my contributions significantly advanced the team's comprehension of the topic, not only to reach a quota. I was able to investigate various viewpoints and approaches used by other scholars in relevant domains by looking at six related papers. I now have a more comprehensive grasp of the subject and was able to spot any gaps that our study may fill. While some of the literature emphasized novel strategies, others offered insightful analysis of potential problems that helped us to better focus our research. I felt gratified when I went above and beyond the call of duty because it made me feel like I owned my contribution to the project. It also reaffirmed how crucial initiative and diligence are in group situations. Making this contribution improved the literature review's overall quality and increased my interest in the study. It was a worthwhile experience that demonstrated how crucial individual work is to attaining group success.
